MA 574 (F25) — Binomial & Poisson Distributions

Problem 1 — Binomial Distribution (Fair Coin, 10 Tosses)
Setup

Let \(X\) = number of heads in 10 tosses of a fair coin. Then \[ X \sim \text{Binomial}(n=10,\; p=0.5). \] The probability mass function is \[ P(X = k) = \binom{10}{k}(0.5)^k(0.5)^{10-k} = \binom{10}{k}\left(\frac{1}{2}\right)^{10} = \frac{\dbinom{10}{k}}{1024}, \quad k = 0,1,\ldots,10. \]

(a) Exactly 4 heads

\[ P(X = 4) = \binom{10}{4}\left(\frac{1}{2}\right)^{10} = \frac{210}{1024}. \]

\(\displaystyle P(X=4) = \frac{210}{1024} \approx 0.2051\)
(b) At most 3 heads

\[ P(X \le 3) = \sum_{k=0}^{3} \binom{10}{k}\left(\frac{1}{2}\right)^{10} = \frac{1}{1024}\left[\binom{10}{0}+\binom{10}{1}+\binom{10}{2}+\binom{10}{3}\right]. \] \[ = \frac{1 + 10 + 45 + 120}{1024} = \frac{176}{1024}. \]

\(\displaystyle P(X \le 3) = \frac{176}{1024} \approx 0.1719\)
(c) At least 7 heads

\[ P(X \ge 7) = \sum_{k=7}^{10} \binom{10}{k}\left(\frac{1}{2}\right)^{10} = \frac{\binom{10}{7}+\binom{10}{8}+\binom{10}{9}+\binom{10}{10}}{1024}. \] \[ = \frac{120 + 45 + 10 + 1}{1024} = \frac{176}{1024}. \]

Note: By symmetry of the fair coin (\(p = 0.5\)), \(P(X \ge 7) = P(X \le 3)\).

\(\displaystyle P(X \ge 7) = \frac{176}{1024} \approx 0.1719\)
(d) Between 3 and 6 heads (inclusive)

\[ P(3 \le X \le 6) = \sum_{k=3}^{6}\binom{10}{k}\left(\frac{1}{2}\right)^{10} = \frac{\binom{10}{3}+\binom{10}{4}+\binom{10}{5}+\binom{10}{6}}{1024}. \] \[ = \frac{120 + 210 + 252 + 210}{1024} = \frac{792}{1024}. \]

Alternatively: \(P(3 \le X \le 6) = 1 - P(X \le 2) - P(X \ge 7)\) \(= 1 - \tfrac{56}{1024} - \tfrac{176}{1024} = \tfrac{792}{1024}.\)

\(\displaystyle P(3 \le X \le 6) = \frac{792}{1024} \approx 0.7734\)

Problem 2 — Poisson Distribution (Call Center, \(\lambda = 4\) calls/hour)
Setup

Let \(X\) = number of calls received in one hour. Then \[ X \sim \text{Poisson}(\lambda = 4). \] The probability mass function is \[ P(X = k) = \frac{e^{-4}\,4^k}{k!}, \quad k = 0, 1, 2, \ldots \] Note: \(e^{-4} \approx 0.018316.\)

(a) Exactly 6 calls

\[ P(X = 6) = \frac{e^{-4}\,4^6}{6!} = \frac{e^{-4} \times 4096}{720}. \] \[ = e^{-4} \times 5.6\overline{8} \approx 0.018316 \times 5.6\overline{8} \approx 0.1042. \]

\(\displaystyle P(X=6) = \frac{4096\,e^{-4}}{720} \approx 0.1042\)
(b) At most 2 calls

\[ P(X \le 2) = P(X=0) + P(X=1) + P(X=2) = e^{-4}\left(\frac{4^0}{0!} + \frac{4^1}{1!} + \frac{4^2}{2!}\right). \] \[ = e^{-4}(1 + 4 + 8) = 13\,e^{-4}. \] \[ \approx 13 \times 0.018316 \approx 0.2381. \]

\(\displaystyle P(X \le 2) = 13\,e^{-4} \approx 0.2381\)
(c) At least 1 call

Use the complement: \[ P(X \ge 1) = 1 - P(X = 0) = 1 - \frac{e^{-4}\,4^0}{0!} = 1 - e^{-4}. \] \[ \approx 1 - 0.018316 \approx 0.9817. \]

\(\displaystyle P(X \ge 1) = 1 - e^{-4} \approx 0.9817\)
