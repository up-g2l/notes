Gamma Distribution

A continuous distribution for waiting times and positive, skewed data

Overview

The Gamma distribution is a two-parameter family of continuous probability distributions defined on the positive real line (x > 0). It generalizes the Exponential distribution and is closely related to the Chi-squared distribution.

It models the total waiting time until a fixed number of independent events occur, making it fundamental in reliability engineering, queueing theory, hydrology, and Bayesian statistics.

Probability Density Function

f(x; α, β) = xα−1 e−x/β / (βα Γ(α)),   x > 0
α (shape) — number of events; α > 0
β (scale) — average time between events; β > 0
Γ(α) — the Gamma function; Γ(n) = (n−1)! for positive integers

Key Properties

Mean
αβ
Variance
αβ²
Mode
(α−1)β, α ≥ 1
Skewness
2 / √α
Support
x ∈ (0, ∞)
MGF
(1−βt)−α

Gamma PDF shapes for β = 1 and varying α. As α increases, the distribution becomes more symmetric and bell-shaped.

Examples

Ex 1 Customer Service Call Times

Calls arrive at a call center and each agent handles one call at a time. The time to resolve a single call follows an Exponential(β = 5 min). The total time to handle α = 3 consecutive calls follows a Gamma(α=3, β=5) distribution.

Mean total time = αβ = 3 × 5 = 15 minutes

Variance = αβ² = 3 × 25 = 75  ⟹  SD ≈ 8.66 min

Insight: The Gamma distribution is the sum of α independent Exponential(β) random variables.

Ex 2 Rainfall Modeling

Monthly rainfall totals in a semi-arid region are modeled as Gamma(α=2, β=15 mm).

Expected rainfall = 2 × 15 = 30 mm/month

Standard deviation = √(2 × 225) ≈ 21.2 mm

The positive skew of the Gamma is ideal here: rainfall can't be negative, occasional months can have very high totals.

Ex 3 Bayesian Inference (Conjugate Prior)

In Bayesian statistics, if the likelihood is Poisson(λ), then the conjugate prior for λ is a Gamma distribution. Suppose we observe 12 events over 3 time units. With a Gamma(1, 1) prior, the posterior is Gamma(α = 1+12 = 13, β updated).

The Gamma is the natural prior for rate parameters — it is closed under Poisson updating, making inference analytically tractable.

Special Cases

Exponential Distribution Gamma(α = 1, β) — time until the first event
Chi-Squared Distribution Gamma(α = k/2, β = 2) — used in hypothesis testing
Erlang Distribution Gamma with integer α — used in telecom/queueing
Normal (approx.) As α → ∞, Gamma → Normal by CLT

When to Use the Gamma Distribution

Use the Gamma distribution when you have continuous, positive data that is right-skewed, particularly when modeling:

waiting times for multiple events · failure/survival times in reliability · precipitation and environmental data · income and insurance claim sizes · posterior distributions of rate parameters in Bayesian analysis
